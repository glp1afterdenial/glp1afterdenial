Side Effects #

Similar to other GLP-1s, but the glucagon component adds some differences:

Common (especially when starting):

  • Nausea (most common, usually improves)
  • Decreased appetite
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Fatigue

Glucagon-related:

  • Mild increase in heart rate (5-10 bpm)
  • May feel more “energized”

Managing side effects:

  • Start at 2mg, not 4mg
  • Increase dose every 4 weeks minimum
  • Stay very hydrated
  • Eat protein even when not hungry (100+ grams/day)
  • Inject at night to sleep through nausea peak

Frequently Asked Questions #

Is retatrutide FDA approved?

No. It’s currently in Phase 3 clinical trials. Potential FDA approval is expected in 2026-2027 if trials are successful.

Is retatrutide stronger than tirzepatide?

Yes, based on clinical trial data. Retatrutide showed ~24% weight loss vs ~22% for tirzepatide. The glucagon receptor adds fat-burning that tirzepatide doesn’t have.

Can I switch from tirzepatide to retatrutide?

Yes. If you’ve plateaued on tirzepatide, retatrutide’s glucagon action may help break through. Start at a lower dose since you’re adding a new receptor.

What’s the best dose for weight loss?

In trials, 8-12mg weekly produced the best results. But start at 2mg and titrate up slowly to minimize side effects.

Does retatrutide increase heart rate?

Slightly. The glucagon component can increase heart rate by 5-10 beats per minute. This usually stabilizes over time. Not recommended if you have heart conditions.

How is retatrutide different from semaglutide?

Semaglutide targets 1 receptor (GLP-1). Retatrutide targets 3 (GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon). More targets = more weight loss + additional benefits like increased energy expenditure.

Retatrutide is the most powerful weight loss peptide we’ve seen yet. The combination of appetite suppression (GLP-1), improved insulin response (GIP), AND increased fat burning (glucagon) produces results that no other medication has matched.

But it’s not for everyone. If you’re new to GLP-1s, start with semaglutide. If you’ve tried tirzepatide and want more, or you have significant weight to lose, retatrutide might be worth exploring.
