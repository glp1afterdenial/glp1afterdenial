Compounded Semaglutide vs Wegovy: What's the Difference?

Same active ingredient, different everything else. Compounded semaglutide and Wegovy both contain semaglutide. The difference is who makes it, how it's regulated, and what it costs. Wegovy is FDA-approved and made by Novo Nordisk. Compounded is made by compounding pharmacies during drug shortages, at a fraction of the price.

If you’re researching semaglutide, you’ve probably noticed a huge price gap between “compounded semaglutide” ($100-250/month) and “Wegovy” ($1,000+/month). What’s the difference? Is compounded safe? Does it work the same?

Let me break it down.

Are They the Same Drug?

Yes and no.

The Active Ingredient

Both contain semaglutide ‚Äî the GLP-1 receptor agonist that reduces appetite, slows gastric emptying, and helps with weight loss. Same molecule.

But the products themselves are different:

Key Differences Explained

1. Who Makes It #

üè• Compounded

Made by compounding pharmacies ‚Äî specialized pharmacies that create customized medications. Quality varies. Legit 503B pharmacies are FDA-registered and inspected. Sketchy ones... aren't.

2. How It’s Regulated #

Wegovy: Full FDA approval. Went through clinical trials, demonstrated safety and efficacy, and is continuously monitored.

Compounded semaglutide: Exists in a gray area. The FDA allows compounding of drugs on the “shortage list.” Semaglutide has been on that list due to high demand. Compounding pharmacies can legally make it, but the final product isn’t FDA-approved.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Shortage List Matters

Compounding pharmacies can only legally make semaglutide because it's on the FDA shortage list. If/when Novo Nordisk catches up with demand, semaglutide could come off the list ‚Äî and compounded versions would become legally questionable.

This is what happened with tirzepatide (Zepbound/Mounjaro) in late 2024 ‚Äî it came off the shortage list, and compounding largely stopped.

3. Format and Delivery #

Wegovy: Pre-filled auto-injector pens. Click a button, it injects. No measuring, no syringes, no math.

Compounded: Typically comes in vials. You’ll need to:

  • Draw the correct amount with a syringe
  • Possibly reconstitute (mix powder with water) depending on the formulation
  • Calculate your dose

Some telehealth platforms send pre-mixed, pre-measured syringes. Others send vials. Ask before you sign up.

4. Consistency #

Wegovy: Every pen is identical. Precise dosing, consistent formulation.

Compounded: Quality depends on the pharmacy. Good 503B pharmacies produce consistent, high-quality products. Bad ones… don’t.

Why the huge difference?

Novo Nordisk spent billions on R&D, clinical trials, and FDA approval. They also have massive marketing budgets and shareholder expectations. Compounding pharmacies skip all of that ‚Äî they just make the molecule.

Does Compounded Work as Well?

For most people, yes.

The active ingredient is the same. If the compounding pharmacy is legitimate and produces quality semaglutide, it should work identically to Wegovy.

What the Science Says

No large clinical trials compare compounded semaglutide to Wegovy directly. But the mechanism is identical ‚Äî same molecule binding to the same receptors. Anecdotally, thousands of people report similar results with compounded versions.

Potential Differences #

Absorption: Some people report slightly different responses when switching between brand and compounded. This could be due to:

  • Different formulations (salt form, inactive ingredients)
  • Inconsistent compounding
  • Placebo effect
  • Normal variation in how batches affect you

Consistency: Wegovy is the same every time. Compounded may vary slightly between batches or pharmacies.

The Risks of Bad Compounding #

When compounding goes wrong, it can be serious:

  • Contamination ‚Äî Bacteria, particles, or other contaminants
  • Wrong concentration ‚Äî Too much or too little active ingredient
  • Stability issues ‚Äî Medication degrades faster than expected
  • Incorrect formulation ‚Äî Using the wrong salt form or additives

This is why pharmacy choice matters. Reputable telehealth platforms use legitimate 503B pharmacies. Sketchy operations might not.

Which Should You Choose?

Decision Framework

Choose Wegovy if:

  • Insurance covers it (or most of it)
  • You want FDA-approved, pharmaceutical-grade medication
  • You prefer pre-filled pens (no measuring)
  • Budget isn't a major constraint
  • You're risk-averse about compounding

Choose Compounded if:

  • Insurance doesn't cover Wegovy
  • Cost is a significant factor ($100-250 vs $1,000+)
  • You're comfortable with a reputable compounding pharmacy
  • You want a prescription but can't afford brand
  • You're okay with vials and syringes

The Honest Reality #

Most people choosing compounded semaglutide are doing so because of cost. If insurance covered Wegovy at $25/month, few would choose compounded instead.

There’s no shame in that. The medication works. Reputable compounding pharmacies produce quality products. Just do your research on the platform and pharmacy.

Frequently Asked Questions #

Is compounded semaglutide legal? Yes, when produced by a licensed compounding pharmacy during an FDA-recognized shortage. The legality depends on the shortage status.

Will compounded semaglutide show up differently on tests? No. It’s the same molecule. Any testing would show semaglutide.

Can I switch from Wegovy to compounded (or vice versa)? Yes. Same active ingredient. You may notice slight differences in how it feels, but the mechanism is identical.

Why doesn’t insurance cover compounded? Insurance typically only covers FDA-approved medications. Compounded drugs aren’t FDA-approved (even if the ingredient is the same).

Is Ozempic the same as Wegovy? Same active ingredient (semaglutide), different dosing and FDA approval. Ozempic is approved for diabetes; Wegovy is approved for weight loss. Higher max dose with Wegovy.

What happens if semaglutide comes off the shortage list? Compounding would become legally questionable. Telehealth platforms would likely stop offering it or pivot to other solutions.

The Bottom Line #

Same drug, different packaging, wildly different prices.

Wegovy is the gold standard ‚Äî FDA-approved, consistent, convenient. But at $1,000+/month without insurance, it's out of reach for many people.

Compounded semaglutide from a reputable source is a legitimate alternative. Do your research on the telehealth platform and pharmacy, and you can get effective medication at a fraction of the cost.
