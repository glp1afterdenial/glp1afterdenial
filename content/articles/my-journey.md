My doctor prescribed Ozempic and insurance covered it. I didn't think much about it at the time.

<li><strong>Food noise stopped</strong> ‚Äî first time in my life I wasn't constantly thinking about food</li>
<li><strong>Inflammation gone</strong> ‚Äî stuff I'd dealt with for years disappeared</li>

Ozempic (semaglutide) was approved for Type 2 diabetes. The weight loss was technically a "side effect." This becomes important later.

<h2 id="insurance-2024" style="border-left: 4px solid #ef4444; padding-left: 16px; margin-top: 48px;">2024: Insurance Stopped Covering It</h2>

As GLP-1s got popular for weight loss, insurers got stricter. Many now only cover them for diabetes, not weight management.

<div style="background: #fef2f2; border-radius: 12px; padding: 20px; margin: 20px 0;">
<h4 style="margin: 0 0 12px 0; color: #dc2626;">‚ùå My Situation</h4>
<ul style="margin: 0; padding-left: 20px; color: #991b1b;">
<li>Insurance stopped covering Ozempic</li>
<li>Prior authorization wasn't even an option with my plan</li>
<li>Policy excluded weight loss medications entirely</li>
</ul>
</div>

So I stopped taking it. Figured I'd maintain with diet and exercise.

**I was wrong.** The weight came back. The food noise returned.

<h2 id="zepbound" style="border-left: 4px solid #22c55e; padding-left: 16px; margin-top: 48px;">Late 2024: Zepbound (Self-Pay)</h2>

After gaining the weight back, I knew I needed to get back on medication. I looked into getting Zepbound covered by insurance.
