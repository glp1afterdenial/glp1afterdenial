<h2 id="what-is-lillydirect" style="border-left: 4px solid #3b82f6; padding-left: 16px;">What Is LillyDirect?</h2>

LillyDirect is Eli Lilly's direct-to-patient program. It lets you get Zepbound (and other Lilly medications) shipped directly to you at lower prices than you'd pay at a regular pharmacy.

<div style="background: #f0fdf4; border-radius: 12px; padding: 20px; margin: 20px 0;">
<h4 style="margin: 0 0 12px 0; color: #15803d;">✓ Key Facts</h4>
<ul style="margin: 0; padding-left: 20px; color: #166534;">
<li><strong>Official program</strong> — Run by Eli Lilly, the company that makes Zepbound</li>
<li><strong>Real medication</strong> — FDA-approved, pharmaceutical-grade Zepbound</li>
<li><strong>No insurance needed</strong> — Self-pay pricing for everyone</li>
<li><strong>HSA/FSA eligible</strong> — Use pre-tax health savings</li>
<li><strong>Requires prescription</strong> — From your doctor or their telehealth partners</li>
</ul>
</div>

**Why does this exist?** Lilly created LillyDirect because the pharmacy middleman system is broken. List prices are insane ($1,000+/month), and insurance coverage is a mess. LillyDirect cuts out the middlemen.

<h2 id="my-experience" style="border-left: 4px solid #0ea5e9; padding-left: 16px;">My Experience Using LillyDirect</h2>

I used LillyDirect for about 5 months during my active weight loss phase with Zepbound.

### The Good

**Ordering was easy.** My doctor wrote the prescription, I signed up, and medication arrived within a week. No insurance dance, no prior authorizations, no pharmacy phone calls.

**The medication worked.** This sounds obvious, but after reading horror stories about sketchy compounded medications, I appreciated knowing I was getting the real thing. Consistent results, consistent side effects (the predictable kind).

**HSA made it tolerable.** Paying $399-449/month hurt less when it was pre-tax dollars from my HSA. Still expensive, but ~30% easier to swallow.

### The Bad

**The cost is brutal long-term.** At $449/month for my maintenance dose (5mg), I was looking at $5,400/year indefinitely. That's a vacation. That's a used car payment. I couldn't justify it forever.

**Dose changes require prescription updates.** Every time I titrated up, I needed my doctor to send a new prescription. Minor hassle, but annoying.

### Why I Eventually Switched

Once I hit my goal weight, I transitioned to research peptides for maintenance. The cost savings ($50-150/month vs $449/month) made sense for long-term use, and I was comfortable with the DIY aspect after months on the medication.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is LillyDirect legitimate?**
Yes. It's run by Eli Lilly, the pharmaceutical company that manufactures Zepbound. It's their official patient access program.

**Can I use insurance with LillyDirect?**
LillyDirect is specifically for self-pay. If your insurance covers Zepbound, you'd typically fill through a regular pharmacy (and possibly get a better price with the Zepbound Savings Card).

**How long does shipping take?**
Usually 3-5 business days. Ships cold-packed with ice packs to keep the medication stable.

**Can I get a 90-day supply?**
Currently, LillyDirect offers monthly supplies only.

**What if I need to change doses?**
Your doctor needs to send a new prescription for the new dose. Then you can update your LillyDirect order.

**Is there a commitment or subscription?**
No long-term commitment. You can order month-to-month and stop anytime.
