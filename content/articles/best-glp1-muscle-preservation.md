## The Muscle Loss Problem

Every GLP-1 causes some muscle loss. It's unavoidable when you're in a calorie deficit. But how much muscle you lose varies by medication:

| Medication | Total Weight Loss | Lean Mass Loss | Fat Mass Loss |
|------------|------------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Semaglutide** | ~15-17% | ~39% of loss | ~61% of loss |
| **Tirzepatide** | ~20-22% | ~25% of loss | ~75% of loss |
| **Retatrutide** | ~24% | ~33% of loss | ~67% of loss |

*Data from JAMA viewpoint analysis of Phase 2/3 trials*

**Translation:** If you lose 50 lbs on semaglutide, roughly 20 lbs could be muscle. On tirzepatide, it's closer to 12-13 lbs. 

That's a meaningful difference ‚Äî especially if you're lifting or doing CrossFit.

## The Research

A recent meta-analysis in *ScienceDirect* found that higher-dose semaglutide and tirzepatide were actually the **least effective** at preserving lean mass, despite being the most effective for total weight loss.

The only GLP-1 that showed significant weight loss *without* significant lean mass reduction was liraglutide ‚Äî but it only produces ~6-8% weight loss, which isn't enough for most people.

## What Actually Preserves Muscle

No matter which GLP-1 you use, these factors matter more than the medication itself:

### 1. Protein Intake

<div style="background: #fef2f2; border-radius: 12px; padding: 20px; margin: 24px 0;">
<h4 style="margin: 0 0 12px 0; color: #dc2626;">üéØ Target: 1.6-2.2g protein per kg body weight</h4>
<p style="margin: 0; color: #991b1b;">That's roughly <strong>0.7-1g per pound</strong>. For a 180lb person, aim for 130-180g protein daily.</p>
</div>

A case series published in *PMC* followed three patients who prioritized protein (1.6-2.3g/kg relative to fat-free mass) during GLP-1 treatment. Results:
- One lost only 8.7% of weight as lean mass
- Two actually **gained** lean mass while losing fat

**The key:** Even when you're not hungry, eat protein. GLP-1s crush appetite, but your muscles still need fuel.

### 2. Resistance Training

The same case series showed patients doing resistance training 3-5 days/week preserved significantly more muscle than typical trial participants.

**Minimum effective dose:**
- 2-3 resistance sessions per week
- Hit each muscle group twice
- Progressive overload (gradually increase weight/reps)

### 3. Slower Titration

Rapid weight loss = more muscle loss. Going slower gives your body time to adapt.

Instead of rushing to max dose:
- Stay at each dose 4-6 weeks (not 4)
- Only increase if weight loss stalls
- Consider staying at a moderate dose long-term
