### 2. How It's Regulated

**Wegovy:** Full FDA approval. Went through clinical trials, demonstrated safety and efficacy, and is continuously monitored.

**Compounded semaglutide:** Exists in a gray area. The FDA allows compounding of drugs on the "shortage list." Semaglutide has been on that list due to high demand. Compounding pharmacies can legally make it, but the final product isn't FDA-approved.

<div style="background: #fef3c7; border-radius: 12px; padding: 20px; margin: 20px 0;">
<h4 style="margin: 0 0 12px 0; color: #92400e;">‚ö†Ô∏è The Shortage List Matters</h4>
<p style="margin: 0; color: #78350f;">Compounding pharmacies can only legally make semaglutide because it's on the FDA shortage list. If/when Novo Nordisk catches up with demand, semaglutide could come off the list ‚Äî and compounded versions would become legally questionable.</p>
<p style="margin: 12px 0 0 0; color: #78350f;"><em>This is what happened with tirzepatide (Zepbound/Mounjaro) in late 2024 ‚Äî it came off the shortage list, and compounding largely stopped.</em></p>
</div>

### 3. Format and Delivery

**Wegovy:** Pre-filled auto-injector pens. Click a button, it injects. No measuring, no syringes, no math.

**Compounded:** Typically comes in vials. You'll need to:
- Draw the correct amount with a syringe
- Possibly reconstitute (mix powder with water) depending on the formulation
- Calculate your dose

Some telehealth platforms send pre-mixed, pre-measured syringes. Others send vials. Ask before you sign up.

### 4. Consistency

**Wegovy:** Every pen is identical. Precise dosing, consistent formulation.

**Compounded:** Quality depends on the pharmacy. Good 503B pharmacies produce consistent, high-quality products. Bad ones... don't.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is compounded semaglutide legal?**
Yes, when produced by a licensed compounding pharmacy during an FDA-recognized shortage. The legality depends on the shortage status.

**Will compounded semaglutide show up differently on tests?**
No. It's the same molecule. Any testing would show semaglutide.

**Can I switch from Wegovy to compounded (or vice versa)?**
Yes. Same active ingredient. You may notice slight differences in how it feels, but the mechanism is identical.

**Why doesn't insurance cover compounded?**
Insurance typically only covers FDA-approved medications. Compounded drugs aren't FDA-approved (even if the ingredient is the same).

**Is Ozempic the same as Wegovy?**
Same active ingredient (semaglutide), different dosing and FDA approval. Ozempic is approved for diabetes; Wegovy is approved for weight loss. Higher max dose with Wegovy.

**What happens if semaglutide comes off the shortage list?**
Compounding would become legally questionable. Telehealth platforms would likely stop offering it or pivot to other solutions.
